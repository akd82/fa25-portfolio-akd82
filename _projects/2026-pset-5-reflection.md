Problem Set #5 was the most valuable assignment for me because it connected individual heat transfer concepts into a complete thermal system analysis. In prior assignments, I learned to solve isolated conduction or convection problems, however, this assignment required combining (1) fin theory, fin efficiency and effectiveness, thermal resistance networks, contact resistance, and overall heat sink performance into one workflow, and (2) energy balances and the lumped capacitance method for transient analysis. Working through both the steady-state fin array problem and the transient Joule heating problem improved my ability to justify my assumptions with physical reasoning, such as evaluating the Biot number before applying the lumped method.

In addition, this assignment was particularly valuable because it emphasized thinking at the system level rather than performing isolated calculations. Instead of simply solving for temperature, I had to evaluate how design choices affect overall thermal resistance and device performance. These skills are directly applicable to electronics cooling, aerospace thermal management, and any application where maintaining safe operating temperatures is critical.
